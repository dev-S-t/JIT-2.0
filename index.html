The Micro-Expiry Buffer Concept

1

Why Standard Predictive JIT Fails in Healthcare

Standard Just-in-Time (JIT) works in manufacturing because demand is deterministic. In healthcare, demand is stochastic (random). Existing papers propose JIT using ML (XGBoost/LSTM) to predict demand. However, prediction errors are inevitable.

Result: When the model under-predicts, patients die (shortage). When it over-predicts, wastage occurs. Healthcare cannot tolerate shortages, so hospitals revert to hoarding (Traditional Model).
2

The Innovation: Micro-Expiry as a Confidence Buffer

We introduce a control variable missing from other papers: Resource Investment for Life Extension. Recent FDA guidelines allow extending platelet shelf life (from 5 to 7 days) via secondary bacterial testing.

We don't do this for every unit (too expensive). Our model monitors Prediction Confidence.

  • High Confidence Forecast: Produce exact amount. Standard 5-day expiry. Low cost.
  • Low Confidence / High Volatility: Produce target amount AND trigger "Life Extension" on existing stock.
  • Outcome: The extended-life units act as a safety buffer. If demand spikes (prediction miss), these units are available. If demand is low, they don't expire immediately, giving 2 extra days to be used.
info This approach effectively absorbs the noise in prediction models, making JIT viable for life-critical applications where shortage is not an option.
